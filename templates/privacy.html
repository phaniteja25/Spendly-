Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 13, 2026

1. Information We Collect

When you create a Spendly account, we collect information such as your name, email address, and password. When you use the Service, we also store the expense records you enter, including amounts, categories, dates, and descriptions.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information you provide to operate the Service: creating and securing your account, displaying your expense history, and generating spending summaries and charts. We do not use your financial data to provide investment, tax, or financial advice.

3. Data Storage and Security

Your account and expense data are stored in our systems and protected using reasonable technical and organizational measures. However, no method of electronic storage or transmission is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

4. Data Sharing

We do not sell your personal or financial data. We do not share your information with third parties except where required to operate the Service (such as hosting infrastructure) or where required by law.

5. Cookies and Sessions

Spendly may use cookies or session identifiers to keep you signed in and remember your preferences. These are used solely to support core functionality of the Service.

6. Your Choices

You may update or delete the expense data associated with your account at any time. You may also request deletion of your account and associated data by contacting us.

7. Children's Privacy

Spendly is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

8. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Continued use of the Service after changes are posted constitutes your acceptance of the revised policy.

9. Contact

If you have questions about this Privacy Policy or how your data is handled, please reach out through the contact details provided on our website.
